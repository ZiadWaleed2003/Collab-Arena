PROBLEM:
A company has 3 factories producing widgets. Factory A produces 100 widgets/day, Factory B produces 150 widgets/day, and Factory C produces 200 widgets/day. If the company needs to produce 10,000 widgets in the minimum number of days, how should they allocate production?

   - Factory A: 100 widgets/day  
   - Factory B: 150 widgets/day  
   - Factory C: 200 widgets/day  
   - **Total (all factories)**: 100 + 150 + 200 = **450 widgets/day**.

     $ 22 \text{ days} \times 450 \text{ widgets/day} = 9,900 \text{ widgets} $.  
   - Remaining widgets needed: $ 10,000 - 9,900 = 100 $.

3. **Final Day Allocation**:  
   - On day 23, only Factory A is needed to produce the remaining 100 widgets.

**Total Days Required**: **23 days**.  
**Allocation Summary**:  
- **Factories A, B, and C** operate for **22 days**.  
- **Factory A alone** operates on the **23rd day**.  

This strategy ensures the highest possible daily output (450 widgets) for the first 22 days, minimizing the total number of days required.
